# Plans

> Future scope: this capability is part of RelayHQ’s long-term vision and is not implemented yet.

## What it is

Plans are higher-level work containers that break a goal into tasks, milestones, and dependencies across one or more projects.

Example:

```text
Plan: Launch customer onboarding
- Task: write onboarding flow
- Task: draft approval checklist
- Task: assign agent follow-up
```

## Why RelayHQ needs it

RelayHQ needs plans so teams can coordinate larger efforts without losing the task-level control plane.

Plans help teams:

- turn intent into execution
- see the shape of work before it becomes urgent
- manage dependencies and sequencing
- keep humans and agents aligned on the same goal

## Who uses it

- project leads
- operators
- team managers
- agents that need structured work breakdowns

## How it fits the control-plane vision

Plans sit above tasks and give RelayHQ a durable coordination layer for multi-step work.

RelayHQ is not trying to replace the tools that do the work. It should manage the structure around the work:

- what exists
- what depends on what
- who owns each piece
- what needs approval

## Boundaries / non-goals

Plans are not:

- a full project management suite
- a Gantt chart product
- a replacement for execution tools
- a generic document editor
